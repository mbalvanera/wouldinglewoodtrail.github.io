Inglewood is a labyrinth of sights and sounds circumscribed by a geographical perimeter containing places held in the memory by people of all ages and backgrounds, creating a collectively overlapping sense of time as the city evolves. Would Inglewood memorializes the quotidian, blending its land- and sound-scapes, and is created out of things that are already here. As the city is a collection of found objects, spaces and experiences, the work invites people to imagine Would Inglewood through the artists’ lens and their own. With five different public events marking Inglewood’s seasonal changes throughout the year, Would Inglewood is an artistic response to the idea of Inglewood, both collective and personal. The Agee Playhouse in Edward Vincent Jr. Park is both the site and focal point of Would Inglewood  The theater is activated by an evolving installation integrating video and audio works, mixed media painting and sculptural elements, live musical and theatrical performances, group activities and interactive components.

Inglewood es un laberinto audiovisual circunscrito por un perímetro geográfico que contiene lugares dentro en la memoria de las personas de todas las edades y trasfondos, creando una escena colectiva sobrepuesta conforme evoluciona la ciudad. Would Inglewood conmemora lo cotidiano, mezclando su tierra, el sonido ambiental, y creando a partir de lo que ya existe. Así como la ciudad es una colección de objetos encontrados, espacios y experiencias, el trabajo invita a lxs participantes a imaginar Would Inglewood a partir de su mirada y la de lxs artistas. Con cinco diferentes eventos públicos, los que marcan los cambios de estaciones a través del año, Would Inglewood es una respuesta artística a la idea de Inglewood, tanto colectivamente como de manera personal. La Agee Playhouse en el Edward Vincent Jr. Park es tanto el lugar como el punto de enfoque de Would Inglewood. El teatro es activado por una instalación que envuelve integrando trabajos de video y audio, pinturas de técnicas mixtas y elementos escultóricos, música en vivo y actuación teatral, actividades en grupo y componentes interactivos.

Would Inglewood is sited at the Willie Agee Playhouse, which is located in Edward Vincent Jr. Park at 714 Warren Lane, Inglewood. After having been dark and unused for some 15 years, the playhouse— an intimate, 55-seat theater—was reopened after renovation in June 2016, and renamed to honor long-time Inglewood resident Willie Agee.

The artists have spent time exploring the small theater and its environs, noting its high, raftered ceilings and proximity to the park’s swimming pool and basketball court. They have reimagined it as a fluid installation that transitions with the life of the city and park, a magical, unexpected space where play becomes a multifarious entity. Activated by the artists’ collaboration, the playhouse transforms into a synchronous experience of the personal and cultural history of Inglewood and its inhabitants.
