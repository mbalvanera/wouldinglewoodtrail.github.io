Inglewood resident, Holly Tempo, received an MFA from the Claremont Graduate University and is known for her paintings and site-specific works. With an active professional career, the artist has shown extensively at exhibition spaces in Southern California, including the California African American Museum, LA Municipal Art Gallery, LACMA Art and Film Lab, POST, LACE, Ruth Bachofner Gallery, Irvine Fine Arts Center, the Riverside Art Museum, and the San Bernardino County Museum of Art; and, internationally in India, Spain, Great Britain, France, The Netherlands, Israel and Central America. The artist is a 2012 recipient of a Pollock-Krasner Foundation Grant. She has taught in the Fine Arts Department at Otis College of Art & Design since 2001. An active member of the local artist community, Tempo has helped to organize the annual Inglewood Open Studios event and worked with other artists to secure the passage of the live/work ordinance for artists in the city.

Daniel Rothman is a composer, sound and installation artist, working in a variety of media. His work has been featured throughout the US and abroad, including the Steirischer Herbst and the Zentrum für Kunst und Medientechnologie (ZKM). His slowly transforming sound and light work Sense Absence, in collaboration with visual artist Paul Tzanetopoulos, was installed in the Hans Otte Raum at the Weserberg Museum (Bremen, Germany), and collaborations with a variety of artists and writers, who include Jim Campbell and Yoko Tawada, have produced works for Los Angeles County Museum of Art and Südwestrundfunks Baden-Baden, among others. His work has been recognized with a National Endowment for the Arts fellowship for the chamber opera Cézanne’s Doubt, available on New World Records, and grants and awards include the Rockefeller and Argosy foundations. For more than a decade he taught on the faculty at CalArts in music composition and Critical Studies, and held visiting appointments at Darmstadt Internationales Ferienkurse, Mills College and California College of the Arts.
